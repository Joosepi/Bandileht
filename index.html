system of a down

System of a Down formed in 1994 in Los Angeles, California. The band came together through the Armenian-American community, with Serj Tankian and Daron Malakian meeting at an Armenian youth organization. They initially recruited Shavo Odadjian, who was originally going to be their manager but ended up becoming their bassist. Andy Khachaturian was their original drummer, later replaced by John Dolmayan in 1997.
What makes the band's formation interesting is how their shared Armenian heritage and experiences influenced their musical direction. All four members brought different musical influences - from heavy metal and hard rock to Armenian folk music and progressive rock. Their innovative sound emerged from this unique cultural fusion.
band's early days involved playing shows in the Los Angeles club scene under the name Soil (with a different lineup), before changing to System of a Down, inspired by a poem written by Daron Malakian titled "Victims of a Down."

THE BAND

Serj Tankian

LEAD VOCALIST

DESCRIPTION

Born on August 21, 1967, in Beirut, Lebanon, Serj Tankian is the distinctive voice of System of a Down. His incredible vocal range spans from operatic highs to guttural lows, often switching between styles mid-song.

Daron Malakian

Guitar & Vocals

DESCRIPTION

Born on July 18, 1975, in Hollywood, California, Daron Malakian is the musical architect of SOAD. His innovative guitar work combines Middle Eastern scales with heavy metal riffs, creating the band's signature sound.

Shavo Odadjian

Bass

DESCRIPTION

Born on April 22, 1974, in Yerevan, Armenia, Shavo Odadjian brings his distinctive style to SOAD's rhythm section. His aggressive playing style and theatrical performance approach define the band's heavy sound.

John Dolmayan

Drums

DESCRIPTION

Born on July 15, 1973, in Lebanon, John Dolmayan joined SOAD in 1997. His powerful and precise drumming style combines technical complexity with raw energy, perfectly complementing the band's dynamic range.
