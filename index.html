system of a down

System of a Down formed in 1994 in Los Angeles, California. The band came together through the Armenian-American community, with Serj Tankian and Daron Malakian meeting at an Armenian youth organization. They initially recruited Shavo Odadjian, who was originally going to be their manager but ended up becoming their bassist. Andy Khachaturian was their original drummer, later replaced by John Dolmayan in 1997.

What makes the band's formation interesting is how their shared Armenian heritage and experiences influenced their musical direction. All four members brought different musical influences - from heavy metal and hard rock to Armenian folk music and progressive rock. Their innovative sound emerged from this unique cultural fusion.

Serj Tankian

lead vocalist

description Serj Tankian stands as one of modern metal's most distinctive voices. Born in 1967, he emerged from the Los Angeles rock scene to become System of a Down's frontman, where his operatic vocals and sharp political commentary set him apart. His voice can transform from gentle melodies to intense screams within seconds, while his stage presence combines theatrical flair with raw emotion. Beyond music, he's a passionate activist for Armenian causes, environmental issues, and social justice. Tankian's artistry extends beyond metal - he's composed classical music, written poetry, and created visual art. His lyrics often tackle heavy topics like genocide, war, and corruption, but he's also known for surreal, almost humorous wordplay. Off-stage, he's surprisingly reserved, more intellectual than his energetic performances might suggest. While System of a Down brought him fame, his solo work showcases his versatility, ranging from jazz to electronic to orchestral pieces.

daron malakian

guitar, vocals

Born July 18, 1975, in Hollywood, California, Daron Malakian stands out as one of modern metal's most innovative guitarists. His playing style defies traditional metal conventions, blending Middle Eastern scales, punk aggression, and progressive complexity. As SOAD's primary songwriter, he's the architect behind many of their most famous songs, including "Chop Suey!" and "Toxicity." description Malakian's guitar work is immediately recognizable - he crafts intricate, often unorthodox riffs that somehow remain incredibly catchy. His vocal harmonies with Serj Tankian create the band's distinctive dual-vocal approach, with his more aggressive, raw singing style complementing Tankian's operatic delivery. On stage, he's known for his intense energy and unique fashion sense, often performing in crop tops or elaborate outfits while making wild, theatrical expressions.

Shavo Odadjian

bass

description Shavo Odadjian, born on April 22, 1974, in Yerevan, Armenia, is a multifaceted artist who has made significant contributions to modern metal music. Before becoming System of a Down's bassist, he was initially the band's manager and then joined as a member, contributing to their innovative sound that blends alternative metal with Armenian musical influences. Beyond his work with System of a Down, Odadjian has ventured into various creative projects. He's worked as a music producer and DJ, and has collaborated with artists across different genres. He's also known for his involvement in music video direction, having directed some of System of a Down's videos. Odadjian is also an entrepreneur, having launched his own record label and cannabis brand. His playing style combines punk rock energy with complex rhythmic patterns. As an Armenian-American artist, he's been vocal about Armenian causes and has participated in various humanitarian efforts.

John Dolmayan

drums

description John Dolmayan is an Armenian-American musician best known as the drummer for the metal band System of a Down (SOAD). Born on July 15, 1972, in Beirut, Lebanon, Dolmayan and his family emigrated to the United States during the Lebanese Civil War, settling in Los Angeles, California. He joined System of a Down in 1997, shortly before they released their self-titled debut album in 1998, and has been with the band ever since. Dolmayan is widely recognized for his technically proficient drumming style, which blends complex rhythms, fast patterns, and a wide range of influences from rock, metal, jazz, and even traditional Armenian music.
